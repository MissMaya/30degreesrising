1979

The start of the AMAP study and the year of the first World Climate Conference. By this point, modern debates on man-made climate change have been raging for over 80 years, since Swedish Scientist Svante Arrhenius first published his theory that the burning of fossil fuels would lead to an increase in the amount of atmospheric carbon dioxide. Arrhenius posited that increasing CO2 would lead to a trapping of heat and an increase in surface temperatures: a demonstration of the so-called greenhouse effect.
